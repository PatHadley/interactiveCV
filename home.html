Aug 2016

Open Collections Developer - York Museums Trust

This project followed on from the Wikimedia roles at York Museums Trust. YMT's management identified Intellectual Property Rights as the cause of the biggest obstacles to releasing open content: essentially, fear of infringing IPR makes it's impossible to effectively offer the public content through digital channels. I was commissioned to investigate the current IPR landscape within the collections and provide a 'road map' for the future of digital openness at YMT
achievements
  • Bulk surveyed ~170,000 collections items to identify relevant collections
  • Built a prototype IPR license assignment tool for use by curators (in Typeform)
  • Produced a report to inform future IPR policy at YMT
lessons
  • The IPR complexities in the YMT collection turned out to be far greater than anyone anticipated
  • The first two decades of the internet has generated a situation in which very few cultural organisations are confidant about IPR
  • Organisations that are taking risks with their online work are reaping huge benefits

This role with YMT's communications team had me working to develop and maintain YMT's websites and social media channels. June 2014

Yorkshire Wikimedia Ambassador - York Museums Trust/Wikimedia UK

As Yorkshire’s Wikimedia Ambassador I worked with Museums Development Yorkshire to coordinate a county-wide project helping accredited museums engage with Wikipedia. This is part of a global initiative (GLAMwiki) helping galleries, libraries, archives and museums to work with Wikipedia, thus joining publicly/charitably funded knowledge repositories with the world’s 6th-most-popular website. The range of tasks involved was huge, requiring a proportionally diverse skillset. I was tasked with negotiating relationships between the museums - ranging from the National Media Museum to rural village-hall museums - and the Wikimedia community.
achievements
  • Trained more than 50 people to edit Wikipedia
  • Helped three new museum organisations donate content to Wikimedia Commons
  • Built on the previous project at YMT leading to millions of views of YMT images and a strong legacy of curators wanting to open up collections online
lessons
  • Even if you're offering organisations free benefits, negotiating relationships can be slow and tricky
  • Attitudes of openness and 'perpetual beta' are difficult to instil in older organisations

Oct 2013

Wikipedian-in-Residence - York Museums Trust/Wikimedia UK

A Wikipedian-in-Residence works with institutions (normally galleries, libraries, archives or museums) to help them build relationships with Wikipedia and its sister projects. I worked to help York Museums Trust (YMT) share its digital collections, train staff and volunteers, and work with the Wikipedia community. I was also involved in the development of digitisation strategies and the careful management of digital objects. My contributions have aided in the development of YMT’s new public, online collection.

I worked with numerous teams in YMT's institutions (York Art Gallery, York Castle Museum, the Yorkshire Museum) to identify collections suitable for use on Wikipedia and help open up YMT’s knowledge and resources.
achievements
  • Delivered a diverse range of content fro YMT's collection to Wikimedia Commons
  • Became a flagship GLAMwiki project for WikimediaUK
  • Contributed to new open policies for YMT and online collection strategy
lessons
  • Digital projects can be difficult to scale up in organisations with limited resources
  • It is difficult to introduce new workflows to specialists with full schedules
  • The complexities of varied data types rarely allow easy transfers between systems

Mar 2010

Director - Enkyad Heritage Media

Enkyad Heritage Media was a start-up company aiming to produce narrative 3D animations for archaeological and heritage sites. I led a team with illustrator Glenn Hustler and worked with dancers, writers, animators and other archaeologists on a pilot project based on the Mesolithic site of Star Carr.

We were selected to be hosted by the University of Bradford’s business incubator and were supported by the University and city council.

The team worked hard to develop the animation and built good relationships with heritage and arts organisations around Yorkshire. Unfortunately, we never gained sufficient funding to complete the project. The project was caught between arts and heritage organisations and I learned a great deal about the mechanics and subtleties of the funding processes in both.

The challenges of the project forced me to develop better entrepreneurship and interpersonal skills, and be reliant on my own determination.
achievements
  • The project was funded by the University of Bradford's Business Incubation Unit
  • We were a child project of Bradford UNESCO City of Film
  • We began partnerships with Scarborough and York Museums Trusts
lessons
  • Judge collaborators on their specific talents and delegate tasks accordingly
  • Innovating between traditional sectors is a major challenge
  • When working in small teams it's easy to spread oneself too thinly

Sep 2011

PhD (not completed): The Meso-what? Project - University of York

This project was dedicated to learning why a significant period of British prehistory - the Mesolithic - has virtually no public recognition or understanding. Building on this, the project intended to develop strategies and tools to help engage the public with Mesolithic archaeology.

After approximately 18 months I took a leave of absence from the project feeling frustrated with the limited social scope of its design and the restrictions of academia for innovative public engagement. Unfortunately, my reflections during this time were compounded by the death of my mother. Ultimately, I chose to withdraw from the PhD in order to explore other opportunities and ways of working.
achievements
  • Successfully won two full scholarships for the project
  • Presented at two world-class international conferences
  • Conducted two research trips to museums in central Europe and Scandinavia
lessons
  • That some of my ambitions and work preferences don't gel with academia
  • The importance of freedom to pursue my own methods on my own projects
  • That I tend to work best with collaborators rather than alone

MA Mesolithic Studies - University of York

Though the core of this course focused on the Mesolithic period itself (the last hunter-gatherers in Europe), I took parallel modules sufficient to have completed the programme in Archaeological Information Systems. Further, I wrote my thesis on how web-based tools might help Mesolithic scholars share data, communicate and collaborate more effectively.
achievements
  • Gained a distinction in both the taught and research portion of the course
  • Co-ran a conference and workshop for archaeology students across Northern England
  • Rebuilt the web-presence and started social media channels for the journalMesolithic Miscellany
lessons
  • Given the right support and circumstances I can excel
  • My interest in archaeology and the past is couched in a hope to solve problems in the present
  • I am keen to use all forms of media to present and process ideas

Sep 2005

BSc Archaeology (First Class Hons.) - University of Bradford

University of Bradford offers an archaeology programme with particular strengths in archaeological practice and scientific methods. It gave me an excellent overview of current archaeological knowledge, particularly in the UK but also more widely. My undergraduate thesis investigated the history and practice of visualisations and reconstruction images of the Mesolithic. As part of this, I worked with an animator to produce a pilot project which developed into the Enkyad Heritage Media start-up project.
achievements
  • Won the departmental J.J. Raper prize for the best dissertation
  • Also gained a Diploma in Professional Archaeology as a result of a one-year placement
  • Was president of the University Climbing Club
lessons
  • My learning style makes me much more suited to collaborative, short projects rather than solo working
  • I began to become frustrated by the limited and elitist forms of academic communication, this has shaped my work since
